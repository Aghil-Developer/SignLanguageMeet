About MOUNA

A mission-driven platform built to bridge the communication gap between deaf and hearing communities through technology.

Our Mission

MOUNA was born from a simple yet powerful idea: everyone deserves to communicate freely. Over 466 million people worldwide have disabling hearing loss, and many rely on sign language as their primary form of communication.

Our platform combines real-time video calling with speech recognition, automatic subtitles, and sign language video playback to create a truly inclusive communication experience.

How It Works

When a hearing user speaks during a video call, MOUNA's speech recognition engine transcribes the speech in real-time. The transcription is displayed as live subtitles and key words are matched to sign language videos that play automatically.

The deaf user sees both the subtitles and the corresponding sign language videos, ensuring nothing is lost in communication. Subtitles can be displayed in English, Telugu, Tamil, or Malayalam.

Technology Stack

Frontend: HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript with a responsive, accessible interface designed with the Vibranium theme.

Video Calling: WebRTC via PeerJS for peer-to-peer, low-latency video communication.

Speech Recognition: Web Speech API for real-time speech-to-text conversion.

Translation: Cloud translation API for multi-language subtitle support.

Backend: Node.js with Express serving the application and PeerJS signaling server.
